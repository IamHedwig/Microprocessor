Technology Specifications

  • Year Developed

    • -Industry Standard Architecture (ISA) is the bus architecture that was introduced as an 8-bit bus with the original IBM PC in 1981. I/O buses have evolved over decades to accommodate the growing complexity of computer systems.


  • Speed (per unit)

    • -Both kinds of I/O bus networks have transmission speeds that range from 1 to 2.5 megabits per second. Nevertheless, in the time it takes a process bus network to provide a single, huge information packet from a single field device, a device bus network can transmit numerous information packets from numerous field devices.


  • Voltages Used

    • -I/O buses have varying voltage levels that vary according on the attached device and interface standard. Stable power delivery and device compatibility are guaranteed by on-board voltage regulators. For instance, USB interfaces often run at 5 volts to supply power to connected devices.


Bus Purpose and Function

The peripheral devices (keyboard, mouse, and flash drives) are connected to the central processing unit (CPU) through input/output, or extension buses. A bridge that is built in the CPU chipset connects the system bus and I/O busses. I/O interfaces are primarily used for data transmission, reception, device enumeration, power management, and hot-swapping.
